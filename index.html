Purchasing
Bottleneck
  • Manually cross-checking repetitive and disorganized material requests.

  • Failure to define actual "Need Dates."
Consequence
  • Sub-optimal vendor pricing.

  • Reactive, inefficient supply chain causing friction.

  • Mandate clear technical specs and true "Need Dates."
  • Cultivate a data-driven, process-oriented professional culture.

Logistics (Import/Export)
Bottleneck
  • Correcting inaccurate export documentation (weights, CBM).
  • Missing vessel schedules and critical payment deadlines.
Rootcause
  • Lack of a locked data system; allows undocumented, manual data changes.
  • Over-reliance on external vendors due to poor internal oversight.
Consequence
  • Severe shipping and customs clearance delays.
  • High risk of compliance penalties, leading to immediate blame-shifting.
Suggestion
  • Deploy a locked, traceable system for all shipping documents.
  • Enforce strict accountability and clear compliance policies.
  • Standardize workflows to eliminate the need for investor micro-management.
05
Production
Bottleneck
  • Redundant dual-data entry (Excel vs. MISA).
  • Daily firefighting of material mismatches and shortages.
Rootcause
  • Absence of a strictly enforced centralized data system.
  • Reliance on single-source suppliers for critical parts (e.g., mechanical).
  • Lack of standardized export data protocols.
Consequence
  • Operational delays and inefficient output.
  • Untraceable errors in critical export documents (CBM, weights, packing lists).
  • Complete lack of accountability for system errors.
Suggestion
  • Deploy a unified, company-wide data system with digitized approvals.
  • Establish stable company policies to reduce investor micro-management.
  • Transferring HR and Purchasing to Production management.
06
Warehouse
Bottleneck
  • Extensive physical recounting of individual inventory items.
  • Struggling to operate with severely faulty, hazardous equipment.
Rootcause
  • Unstandardized units of measurement across departments (buying in kg vs. counting in pieces).
  • Delayed maintenance execution for critical machinery.
Consequence
  • Crippled material flow to Production.
  • Immediate, high-risk safety liability (forklift electrical/brake failure).
Suggestion
  • Immediate authorization for equipment repair (forklift).
  • Stop using MISA & use 1 system for real-time inventory reconciliation.
  • Unify the import/export reporting system and review working hours (Saturday off).
