1. DATA COLLECTION

A. Survey

The following findings represent the unfiltered voices of the employees. This data is derived directly from self-reported surveys, presented exactly as submitted without any alteration.

01
Accounting & Finance
Bottleneck
  • Constant recalculation and rework of product costing.
Rootcause
  • Inaccurate data input from the Warehouse (incorrect cost allocations, mismatched quantities).
  • Lack of cross-departmental collaboration.
Consequence
  • Delayed financial closing and reporting.
  • Preventing the Board of Directors from accessing timely, accurate financial health metrics.
Suggestion
  • Establish a clearly defined organizational chart with strict boundaries of authority (especially for the Factory Management role).
  • Foster a fair, objective, and collaborative work environment.
02
Human Resources
Bottleneck
  • Manual payroll reconciliation.
  • Continuous internal conflict resolution.
Rootcause
  • Undefined management boundaries allowing cross-departmental interference.
  • Rigid systems and below-market compensation structures.
Consequence
  • A toxic, uncooperative workplace culture.
  • High turnover of essential male workers.
  • Severe organizational vulnerability.
Suggestion
  • Implement capable, data-driven leadership and a standardized operating system.
  • Urgently clarify roles to stop cross-departmental interference.
  • Adjust compensation structures to retain talent.
03
Purchasing
Bottleneck
  • Manually cross-checking repetitive material requests.
  • Processing fragmented, urgent Purchase Orders.
Rootcause
  • Production bypassing standard lead times.
  • Submitting requests without clear technical specifications (SPEC).
  • Failing to define actual "Need Dates."
Consequence
  • Sub-optimal vendor pricing.
  • Increased logistics costs (missed free-shipping thresholds).
  • A highly reactive, inefficient supply chain.
Suggestion
  • Standardize the Material Request process, mandating clear technical specs and true "Need Dates."
  • Cultivate a professional culture where decisions are driven by data and established processes rather than individual interventions.
04
Production
Bottleneck
  • Redundant dual-data entry (Excel vs. MISA).
  • Daily firefighting of material mismatches/shortages.
Rootcause
  • Absence of a strictly enforced centralized data system.
  • Reliance on single-source suppliers (e.g., mechanical parts).
  • Lack of standardized export data protocols.
Consequence
  • Operational delays.
  • Untraceable errors in critical export documents (CBM, weights, packing lists).
  • Complete lack of accountability when errors occur.
Suggestion
  • Deploy a unified, company-wide data system with digitized approvals to reduce dependency on investor micro-management.
  • Establish stable company policies and evaluate a 5-day workweek to optimize overhead and boost morale.
05
Warehouse
Bottleneck
  • Extensive physical recounting of individual inventory items.
  • Struggling with severely faulty equipment.
Rootcause
  • Unstandardized units of measurement across departments (e.g., Purchasing buys in kg, Warehouse counts in pieces).
  • Delayed maintenance execution.
Consequence
  • Crippled material flow to Production.
  • Critical Risk: Immediate, high-risk safety liability due to the forklift's electrical and brake failure.
Suggestion
  • Immediate authorization for equipment repair (forklift) and direct system access (MISA) for real-time inventory reconciliation.
  • Unify the import/export reporting system and review baseline compensation and working hours (Saturday off) for operational staff.
06
Logistics (Import/Export)
Bottleneck
  • Constantly correcting inaccurate finished goods data (weights, CBM) on Invoices and Packing Lists.
  • Missing vessel schedules and critical payment deadlines for import/export operations.
Rootcause
  • Absence of a centralized system; critical data is manually entered and arbitrarily changed by Production or Partners without cross-departmental notification.
  • Complete dependence on external logistics service providers due to a lack of internal information ownership and oversight.
Consequence
  • Severe delays in import/export clearance and shipping operations.
  • High vulnerability to state/customs penalties, leading to immediate blame-shifting because no one is held accountable.
Suggestion
  • Implement a strict, traceable data system for all shipping documentation to prevent unauthorized modifications.
  • Establish clear accountability and definitive reward/penalty policies for export compliance.
  • Build standardized, self-sustaining workflows to prove operational efficiency and reduce the need for investor micro-management.
